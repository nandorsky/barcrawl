A tradition unlike any other.
A trip back through time to a simpler place.

It is with great pleasure and overly intense anticipation that we announce the 3rd Annual Post-Grad Time Machine Bar Crawl will be Saturday, May 10th. For those who have partaken in this adventure before, welcome back. For those joining us for the first time, may God have mercy on your soul.

The Post-Grad Time Machine Bar Crawl began as a way for a few George Washington University alums to relive college days in their old college bars. What may have started as a small blackout gathering has grown to hundreds of degenerates traveling across our nation's capitol on an armada of school buses. Welcome participants not only include GWU alums from DC and around the country, but more importantly - we've been joined by our friends, family, coworkers and random acquaintances outside of the GW family.

PAST MVPs
Year after year, Time Machine participants have brought out their A-Game for this 12- hour, 4 bar journey. To commemorate those who go above and beyond, an MVP award is given out to that person(s) who most exemplify what this day is about: heroism and degeneracy.

Kevin Hall

2012

In the days of the 5 stop bar crawl, Hall was one of two people to make it to all stops, as well as a few pit stops including GW Deli, George's Falafel, and so on.

Michael Potolicchio

2012

While Poto only made it to a few of the stops on the 2012 Bar Crawl, his actions in that time were truly heroic. As folks left the Waterfront, Michael saw a small child fall into the fountain, and with his cellphone in his pocket and without any hesitation, Michael dove into the fountain and saved said small child. A true American Hero.

Nate Andorsky

2013

True leaders step up when adversity presents itself. When Nate stepped aboard a crowded school bus and discovered no radio or sound system, he did not fret - he led. From his leadership with the singing on the bus, to his other activities, throughout the day; by the countless of emails I received in his support as well as the endless amounts Nate chants from 2pm to 2am, Nate was the clear MVP.
